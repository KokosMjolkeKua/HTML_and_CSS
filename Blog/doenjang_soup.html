Korean Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개)

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp doenjang (Korean soybean paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, cubed
  • 100g mushrooms, sliced
  • 200g soft tofu, cubed
  • 1 potato, cubed (optional)

Broth Base:

  • 4 cups anchovy-dashima stock (or vegetable stock)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Ground black pepper to taste

Garnish:

  • Chopped green onions
  • Korean red pepper powder
  • Sesame seeds

Method

  1. Heat sesame oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add doenjang and gochugaru, stir-fry for 1 minute to release the flavors.
  3. Pour in the anchovy stock and bring to a boil.
  4. Add potato if using, simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Add zucchini and mushrooms, cook for 3 minutes.
  6. Gently add tofu and green onions, simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Season with soy sauce and black pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with green onions, red pepper powder, and sesame seeds.

Tips & Notes

For the best flavor, use authentic Korean doenjang. The soup should be slightly thick but still brothy. You can adjust the spiciness by varying the amount of gochugaru used.

Recipe Rating: ★★★★★

A comforting Korean soup that's both nutritious and deeply flavorful. Perfect for cold days!

About Doenjang Jjigae

Doenjang Jjigae is a fundamental Korean soup made with fermented soybean paste. It's considered a staple in Korean cuisine and is often served as part of a traditional Korean meal. The fermented soybean paste provides a rich, umami flavor that makes this soup incredibly satisfying.
