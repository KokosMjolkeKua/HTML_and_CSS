Thai Green Curry

Ingredients

Curry Paste:

  • 4 green chilies, deseeded
  • 2 shallots, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, white part only
  • 1 thumb-sized piece galangal
  • 1 bunch coriander, stems and roots
  • 1 tsp kaffir lime zest
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds

Curry:

  • 2 cans coconut milk
  • 500g chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots
  • 1 cup Thai eggplant
  • 1 red chili for garnish
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves

To Serve:

  • Jasmine rice
  • Lime wedges
  • Extra fresh herbs

Method

  1. Make the curry paste: Blend all curry paste ingredients in a food processor until smooth.
  2. Heat a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the thick part of the coconut milk and simmer until it splits (oil separates).
  3. Add the curry paste and fry for 5 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the chicken and stir to coat with the curry paste. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Pour in remaining coconut milk, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Add kaffir lime leaves.
  6. Simmer for 10 minutes, then add bamboo shoots and Thai eggplant.
  7. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until vegetables are tender and chicken is cooked through.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Garnish with sliced red chili and Thai basil.

Tips & Notes

For the best flavor, make the curry paste from scratch. However, you can use store-bought green curry paste as a time-saving alternative. The key is to fry it well in the coconut cream to release the flavors.

Recipe Rating: ★★★★½

A classic Thai dish that brings together the perfect balance of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors.

About Green Curry

Thai Green Curry (Gaeng Keow Wan) is one of Thailand's most beloved dishes. The color comes from green chilies and fresh herbs, making it one of the spicier Thai curries. The coconut milk helps balance the heat, creating a harmonious blend of flavors.
