My Favorite Animals

Raccoon

I've never seen one in real life, but they look adorable.

Raccoons are known for their black "mask" and ringed tail. In Norway, they are considered an invasive species, as they can spread diseases and disrupt ecosystems. They're extremely adaptable and known for raiding garbage cans in urban areas.

Diet

Raccoons eat almost anything, but here are some favorites:
  • Insects
  • Crayfish
  • Reptiles
  • Birds
  • Fruits
  • Berries
  • Nuts
  • Dead animals
  • Garbage

Red Panda

Looks like a mix between a raccoon and a fox.

The red panda is native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, it's not closely related to the giant panda. Red pandas are great climbers and spend most of their time in trees.

Diet

Mostly herbivores, but sometimes omnivorous:
  • Bamboo
  • Fruits
  • Berries
  • Bird eggs
  • Insects

Arctic Fox

Perfectly adapted to the cold.

Arctic foxes have thick fur and a bushy tail that helps them survive freezing temperatures. They live in the Arctic tundra and are skilled hunters and scavengers.

Diet

Opportunistic feeders:
  • Small mammals (like lemmings)
  • Birds
  • Fish
  • Eggs
  • Berries
  • Leftovers from polar bears

Hedgehog

Spiky and adorable!

Hedgehogs are small mammals with a coat of sharp spines. When threatened, they curl into a ball. They are nocturnal and often found in gardens.

Diet

Mainly insectivores:
  • Beetles
  • Caterpillars
  • Worms
  • Frogs
  • Fruits
  • Snails

Koala

They sleep up to 20 hours a day!

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia. They spend most of their lives in eucalyptus trees and feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals.

Diet

Specialized eaters:
  • Eucalyptus leaves
  • Occasionally bark
  • Nothing else!
